When “fast” becomes the requirement: what COVID changed about diagnostics

Dec 13, 2025 • 6 min read • Category: Regulation

COVID didn’t just accelerate diagnostics. It changed what the public expected diagnostics to be: instant, always available, always definitive. Most of that expectation was never realistic.

The real shift: scale + visibility

Laboratory decision-making became visible to everyone at once. Normally, validation decisions are internal. During an emergency, those decisions become public pressure points.

Speed can change. Accountability does not disappear.

Authorization is not the same thing as readiness

Emergency authorization can allow faster rollout of a method — but local environments still matter: staffing, workflow, maintenance, quality systems, and ongoing monitoring.

LabContext framing: Oversight is layered. “Allowed to exist” is different from “performing well here, now.”
