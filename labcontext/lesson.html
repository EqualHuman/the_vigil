Regulation & Validation: a conceptual map

Education • 8–12 min • Suggested pairing: Interactive: EUA rollout

Students often learn regulation as a list of agencies and acronyms. That’s not useful until you have a mental model. This lesson is that model: a way to understand what each layer is trying to protect, and where responsibility sits.

Three layers you can keep straight

A simple way to think about oversight is as layered questions:

  • Authorization/approval: what is allowed to exist and be marketed?
  • Regulatory standards: what must laboratories do to operate and report results responsibly?
  • Accreditation: what additional requirements and inspections may apply?
LabContext rule of thumb: layers can overlap, but they aren’t interchangeable.

Verification vs validation (conceptually)

Instead of memorizing definitions, think in terms of scope:

  • Verification: “This method performs acceptably here under our conditions.”
  • Validation: “This method performs acceptably across intended uses/claims, and we have evidence.”

The details vary by situation and policy — but the conceptual difference is simple: verification is local confidence; validation is broader evidence.

What emergencies change (and what they don’t)

Emergencies can alter timelines and authorization pathways. What they do not remove is the need for:

  • Evidence that performance is acceptable
  • Ongoing quality systems
  • Clear responsibility for results released
Speed can change. Accountability does not disappear.
